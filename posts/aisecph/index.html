Reconnaissance#

Introduction of Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT)#

I was told this course is about reconnaissance. For me, this is a military term rather than a cyber security term. Although the culture of infosec community is heavily influenced by prior military people and government agencies, it’s still inside the umbrella of IT industry.

When I was learning ethical hacking like a decade ago, it was called Footprinting. Which involves port scanning, network mapping and intel collecting. Back in the old days, there is no social media around. So when you looking for information on someone, you can’t just go online and Google search or check your collection of leaked dataset. What you do is to go for a dumpster diving. Yes, we were told by teachers and textbooks to literally collect information in the trash can. I believe it is still effective today but we just don’t have to do that any longer. Because we have better way available.

Which is Open-Source Intelligence, or so called OSINT. There is a link at the bottom, where you can learn more about.

AI-OSINT#

Okay, here the AI kicks in. You can use tools like ChatGPT to generate a perfect profile for your Alias account, and it’s very hard to identify it’s a fake person.

You can also create video and audio contents using Synthesia or D-ID to make it even more real. It can be used in social engineering attack. So if you are like me, the only child stay very far from my family. It’s better to prepare some secrete words with them. Otherwise, if my mom is getting fraud by someone using AI with my voice, you know what would happen…

It can also be used to bypass security check or generating disinformation as a countermeasure to protect something you can’t simply remove from the public.

More Use cases#

Here are some more uses of AI-OSINT. PimEyes is a popular option to find more photos of someone by reverse searching just like tineye. We also need to identify what we find is belong to a real person. This can be hard while the AI is getting stronger.

ChatGPT is still locked without internet access but it will be able to do that sooner or later. By that time, or right now by using GPT4 or the microsoft alternative, you can do AI-OSINT with extra layers of protection from regulations. But this window won’t be long, and please be a decent person, do things legally. You will get caught if your countersurveillance skill is not better than your offensive skills.

And even if you’re doing white hat gigs for helping some entity to do pentesting or bug bounty, that you think you are completely legal and moral and you’re doing the right thing. You can still get into troubles because others don’t think so and the law doesn’t necessarily work for people with good intentions. Unfortunately, sometimes, it can be the opposite.

Use cases#

Basically, these are the things you can do with ChatGPT for now.

Depends on whether you’re on the Blue team or the Red team, you can either find vulnerabilities or do threat hunting with it, Write Exploits or Patches, create Malware code or Incident response plan, generate phishing emails or filtering rules, and deliver payload or get alert from logs.

The new AI language model maybe not as robust as the existed solutions, but it can do much more, or it’s a more so called general AI than the dumb ones. So the new AI does not replace the old ML systems, but enhance and integrate .

Arendt and Heidegger#

Hannah Arendt in her book, The Life of the Mind, said: “The sad truth is that most evil is done by people who never make up their minds to be good or evil.”

According to Martin Heidegger, “The danger of technology does not lie in technology itself. The essence of technology is by no means anything technological.”

Another quote from his book, The Question Concerning Technology, said “Everywhere we remain unfree and chained to technology, whether we passionately affirm or deny it. But we are delivered over to it in the worst possible way when we regard it as something neutral; for this conception of it, to which today we particularly pay homage, makes us utterly blind to the essence of technology.”

He said this right after the second world war which ended up with gas chambers and atomic bombs. Think about the world we’re living now, it’s not far from the great catastrophe in each direction. So, do not tunnel vision on AI itself, use the technology as the means of revealing, uncover what was concealed.

Let’s head back to the meaning about Shinobi. It’s just a traditional way to say ninjia. Which means to bear something, or to suppress, restrain oneself.

Its Chinese character constructs as a heart under blade, 『刃の下に心あり』. To me, the blade is a metaphor of technology, such as tools or weaponry. The heart is a metaphor of our mind, will or spirit. This is the opposite of our rationality, reason, intelligence, or logic—which even a strong AI would never match. That’s the humanity we should preserve and taking care of, not the opposite. The irrational unconscious part of us, such as our being and existence, love and devotion, ethics and determination, faith and religion. These are far from the animal instinct or excessive radical.
